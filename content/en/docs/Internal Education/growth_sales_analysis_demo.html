Background Information

Attached, are recent sales performance for a Sales Organization Enterprise. There are two tabs included:

  • Opportunities Closed Won / Lost - the orange tab contains all deals the sales team closed as either won or lost.
  • Rep Tenures - the blue tab contains a key for the different field sales reps and SDRs (that you will find referred to in the orange tab by Rep or SDR number) with how long they have been in the role.

The head of sales strategy has asked for an annual review of the business.

  1. At the highest level, how did we perform in 2017?
  2. What are specific areas of strength or weakness?
  3. Strategically, how efficient is our sales org (SDRs and direct sellers)?
  4. What other interesting considerations should we think about for 2018?

Investigating Attributes: Correlation

We'll conduct a simple affinity analysis to determine whether there is correlation among the different attributes that may be influencing our deal close. Based on this frequency analysis, we'll be able to see a few trends that would provide direction for further analysis. If there is a positive correlation, this indicates that as the frequency of one attribute occurs, the frequency of the other attribute tends to be present, negative indicates that increased frequency in one attribute is reflected with a decrease in frequency of other attributes.

sql_string = """
            SELECT 
                Stage,
                Country, 
                SDR, 
                Opportunity_Owner, 
                Fiscal_Period, 
                Lead_Source, 
                rank() over(partition by Stage order by Close_Date ASC) as ranking
            FROM new_business_sql
            WHERE Stage in ('Closed Won', 'Closed Lost')
            """

deal_corr= pd.read_sql(sql_string, conn)

#creating a correlation table among the different attributes to identify which ones influence each other.
deal_matrix = deal_corr.apply(lambda x: x.factorize()[0]).corr()
print(deal_matrix)

Field Sales Rep Efficiency

While there's many factors to consider with the sales efficiency and performance, ultimately we'd like to consider the ROI from each sales representative. While information such as the cost of each sales representative isn't available to inform ROI we're able to evaluate the efficiency of each representative in closing a deal. Strategically, since we're evaluating the new business across the two product lines, I'd like to understand the Win Ratio for a Field Sales Representative. If they're provided with a lead, what is their likelihood of closing this deal?

The table above is ranked by Win Ratio. This is calculated as Closed Won/(Deals). There's a mix of information, where tenure isn't a true indicator of whether a Sales Rep is able to close deals through the year. For example, while Rep 3 has the longest tenure and is able to close deals at the highest win ratio, The total volume of deals is less than Rep 9,6,2 - with rep 9 and 6 having started in the middle of 2017. We also observe how Rep 8 has an effiency of 100% with 6 total deals and 6 Total Won recorded.

From what we garner in this, there are some outliers among the Sales Representatives performance. Albeit each has their own methods and personalities, a standardization of playbook so that a Win ratio across all representatives would be a good iniative for 2018. Redistribution of volume of deals could also be of benefit given the large discrepency in total deals. 3 reps hold 100+ deals while 6 reps hold < 25 deals in the year
